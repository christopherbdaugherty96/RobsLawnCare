# Weekly Revenue Targets

## Purpose
Define simple weekly revenue targets for ShortCut so launch planning stays realistic.

## Current Phase
Pre-launch planning. These are planning targets, not current revenue claims.

## Starter Revenue Model
Primary early revenue should come from recurring grass cutting customers.

Power washing should be treated as a secondary add-on after readiness is confirmed.

## Example Weekly Targets
### Stage 0 — Pre-Launch
Target: $0 field revenue

Goal:
- finish launch checklist
- acquire equipment/transport
- prepare website and quote workflow
- validate pricing assumptions

### Stage 1 — Soft Launch
Target: $250–500/week

Goal:
- 5–10 small recurring grass cutting customers
- controlled nearby jobs
- before/after photos
- first reviews

### Stage 2 — Early Route Building
Target: $750–1,250/week

Goal:
- 15–25 recurring customers
- dense route clusters
- basic weekly reporting
- stable quote/follow-up process

### Stage 3 — Strong Part-Time Route
Target: $1,500–2,500/week

Goal:
- recurring route density
- add-on cleanup or power washing jobs only when ready
- predictable weekly schedule

## Tracking Metrics
Track weekly:
- leads received
- quotes sent
- jobs completed
- recurring customers
- one-time jobs
- total revenue
- fuel/equipment costs
- unpaid invoices
- reviews requested
- reviews received

## Operating Rule
Do not chase revenue by accepting unsafe, scattered, underpriced, or unready work.
