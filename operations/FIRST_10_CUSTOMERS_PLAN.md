# First 10 Customers Plan

## Purpose
Define a realistic strategy for acquiring the first recurring ShortCut customers without overexpanding too early.

## Current Phase
Pre-launch planning.

The goal is not rapid scaling.
The goal is building a stable recurring route foundation.

## Target Customer Type
Prioritize:
- nearby residential customers
- subdivisions and dense neighborhoods
- recurring mowing customers
- customers with manageable property sizes
- customers likely to refer neighbors

Avoid early:
- large commercial contracts
- long-distance jobs
- unsafe properties
- underpriced work
- jobs beyond equipment or transport readiness

## Goal Of First 10 Customers
The first 10 customers should help:
- validate pricing
- validate workflow
- test scheduling
- test transport setup
- build reviews
- create before/after content
- establish recurring route density

## Suggested Acquisition Sources
### Friends / Family / Local Network
Use early trusted connections for:
- first jobs
- workflow practice
- before/after examples
- referrals

### Local Visibility
Potential low-cost launch methods:
- neighborhood Facebook groups
- local recommendations
- Google Business Profile
- flyers or door hangers
- neighbor referrals

## Communication Rule
Prioritize:
- fast response times
- clear expectations
- professional communication
- realistic scheduling

Do not:
- overpromise
- claim services not ready
- accept too many scattered jobs early

## Recurring Route Rule
Recurring nearby mowing customers are more valuable than random one-time jobs during early launch.

## Power Washing Rule
Power washing should remain secondary until:
- safety readiness exists
- pricing is validated
- workflow is stable
- equipment is reliable

## Success Indicators
The first 10 customers should produce:
- stable recurring jobs
- operational confidence
- improved workflow
- reviews
- local visibility
- better pricing understanding
- route density foundation
