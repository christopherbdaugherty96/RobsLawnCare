# Power Washing Safety Rules

## Purpose
Define safety boundaries before ShortCut offers power washing services.

Power washing is a planned secondary service line. It should not be offered publicly until equipment, training, pricing, workflow, and safety readiness are confirmed.

## Core Safety Risks
Power washing can cause:
- surface damage
- paint removal
- concrete etching
- water intrusion
- electrical hazards
- slip hazards
- chemical exposure
- property damage
- personal injury

## Before Any Power Washing Job
Confirm:
- requested surface
- surface condition
- water access
- drainage direction
- nearby electrical outlets or fixtures
- fragile materials
- painted or sealed surfaces
- customer expectations
- weather conditions
- required cleaning method

## Do Not Service Without Readiness
Do not perform power washing when:
- equipment is not reliable
- pressure rating is not understood
- surface risk is unclear
- chemicals are required but not understood
- electrical risk is present
- ladder work is required beyond safe ability
- the job exceeds current skill, insurance, or legal readiness

## Surface Caution
Use extra caution with:
- painted surfaces
- wood decks
- siding
- older concrete
- pavers
- brick
- screens
- windows
- roof materials
- sealed surfaces

## Customer Communication Rule
Before starting, explain that some surfaces can react differently to pressure and cleaning methods.

Do not promise perfect restoration.

## Documentation Rule
Take before and after photos when useful and appropriate.

Record notes about surface condition before starting.

## Launch Rule
Power washing should remain gated until ShortCut has a documented safety process, pricing model, service limitations, and damage-response plan.
