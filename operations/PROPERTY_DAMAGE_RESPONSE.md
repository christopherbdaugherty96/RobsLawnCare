# Property Damage Response

## Purpose
Define a calm and professional response process if property damage or customer concerns occur.

## Operating Principle
Do not hide damage, argue emotionally, or make unrealistic promises.

Handle issues professionally, honestly, and calmly.

## Immediate Response Steps
If possible damage occurs:
1. stop work safely
2. document the area
3. take photos when appropriate
4. communicate clearly with the customer
5. avoid admitting legal fault on the spot
6. avoid making promises beyond current ability
7. record notes about what happened

## Customer Communication Rule
Stay calm and professional.

Focus on:
- understanding the concern
- documenting the issue
- discussing reasonable next steps

Do not:
- argue aggressively
- blame the customer emotionally
- ignore concerns
- continue unsafe work

## Power Washing Risk Reminder
Power washing has increased risk for:
- paint damage
- water intrusion
- surface etching
- damaged seals
- damaged landscaping

Use extra caution before accepting unfamiliar surfaces.

## Operational Rule
Do not perform work beyond current:
- equipment readiness
- skill level
- transport capability
- legal readiness
- insurance readiness

As ShortCut grows, this process may eventually connect with:
- insurance workflows

- before/after proof systems
